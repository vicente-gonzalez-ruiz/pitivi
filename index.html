Chapter 1
Introduction

1.1 About Pitivi

  • Pitivi is a free and open source video editor based on the GStreamer Multimedia Framework.
  • It is distributed under LGPL.
  • Runs on Linux, BSD, Windows and Mac.

1.2 Install

1.2.1 Debian

sudo apt−get install pitivi 
 
# or, if the version in the repository is not fresh: 
 
wget http://pitivi.ecchi.ca/bundles/releases/pitivi−0.94−beta−x86_64.tar

1.3 Supported audio/image/video formats

  • Those supported by GStreamer.

1.4 The main window

  1. Header bar: Basic functions.
  2. Ruler: A time scale.
  3. Timeline: Contains the video layers.
  4. Timeline toolbar: Timeline edition.
  5. Playback toolbar: To control the playback.
  6. Previewer: To view your results.
  7. Media and eﬀect libraries.
  8. Clip conﬁguration, transitions and title editor.

Chapter 3
Controlling the timeline

  1. : Select a video clip with the mouse.
  2. : Use the mouse wheel anywhere over the timeline.
  3. : Hold down the Shift key while using the mousewheel.
  4. : Holding the Ctrl key while using the mousewheel over the timeline.
  5. : Click on the ruler.
  6. : Click on the ruler and move the mouse with the button still held down.

Chapter 5
Trimming (cut and paste)

5.1 Basic trimming

  • Trimming is the act of changing the length of a clip by moving its beginning or end point in its timeline.
  • To perform a trimming do:
    1. Move the mouse cursor over the edge of a clip and a trimming handle will appear.
    2. Drag the trimming handle in an appropriate direction to either reduce or increase the length of the clip.

5.2 Ripple editing

  • Ripple editing is a variant of basic trimming which, in addition to trimming a clip, also moves the following clips.
  • To perform a ripple edition do:
    1. Place the mouse cursor on a trimming handle.
    2. Press and hold Shift.
    3. Drag the trimming handle.

5.3 Roll editing

  • Roll editing is also a variant of basic trimming which, in addition to trimming a clip, trims the adjacent clips in a complementary way to prevent creating gaps.
  • To perform a ripple edition do:
    1. Place the mouse cursor on a trimming handle between two adjacent clips.
    2. Press and hold Ctrl.
    3. Drag the trimming handle.

Chapter 6
Keyframe curves

  • A keyframe is a frame (an image) of a video that does not depend on any other frame of the video when decoding.
  • The keyframe curve is a piecewise “curve” with vertices in the keyframes and that allows to change the opacity, eﬀects, volumen, etc. of the clips on both, audio a video.
  • By default, all clips have an hidden fully-ﬂat keyframe curve.
  • Most eﬀects can be controlled with keyframes curves.
  • Keyframe (in yellow) curves appear when clicking on a layer. A vertice can be added by clicking on the keygrame curve.

Chapter 7
Splitting

7.1 Split the current clip

  • Divides the selected clip into two adjacent clips. Notice that if no clips are selected, all the clips under the playhead will be split.
  • To split a single clip:
    1. Click on the clip.
    2. Reposition the playhead (if needed).
    3. Click on the Split button on the timeline toolbar or press S.

7.2 Split all clips at the same time

  • To split all the clips across the all layers:
    1. Click on a blank area of the timeline (this will deselect all selected clips, if any).
    2. Reposition the playhead (the vertical red line of the timeline), if needed.
    3. Click on the Split button on the timeline toolbar or press S.

7.3 Split multiple clips

  • To split multiple selected clips:
    1. Position the playhead where you want to split.
    2. Add or remove clips to the selection with the Ctrl modiﬁer key. This will not aﬀect the playhead’s position.
    3. Click on the Split button on the timeline toolbar or press S.

Chapter 8
Transitions

8.1 Fade-in/out

  • Use the keyframe curve. You need to create for the fade-in an uphill slope and viceversa.

8.2 Crossfading (not implemented)

  • To do a crossfade between two clips on the same layer, simply drag one of the clips onto the other so that it overlaps. By default, a linear transition will be created. A loop mode is also available.

Chapter 9
Eﬀects

  • Click on Effect library.
  • Available eﬀects depend on the software installed on your computer. If you don’t see anything (or only a handful of eﬀects) in the eﬀect library, make sure that the gnome-video-eﬀects package is installed on your system. Alternatively, you can look for packages containing GStreamer or frei0r eﬀect plugins that are available in the repositories of your distribution.

9.1 Adding (applying) eﬀects

  1. Select an existing clip on the timeline.
  2. Choose an eﬀect in the eﬀect library.
  3. Drag the chosen eﬀect into the middle pane -> Clip configuration -> Effects.

9.2 Removing eﬀects

  1. Select an existing clip on the timeline.
  2. Select the eﬀect from the middle pane -> Clip configuration -> Effects.
  3. Click the Remove eﬀect button.

9.3 Toggling eﬀects

  • If you want to temporarily disable an eﬀect (to compare with and without the eﬀect, or for performance reasons), click the corresponding Active checkbox in the middle pane -> Clip configuration -> Effects.
